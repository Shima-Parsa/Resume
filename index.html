Experience

Research Experience

  • Tissue Engineering (2021-present)

During 3 years at the regenerative medicine lab, I developed liver microtissues with a natural human liver cell ratio used to explore the effects of nanoparticles, human Wharton's jelly-derived mesenchymal stem cell (hWJ-MSC) exosomes, and Nicotinamide on fatty liver disease. Additionally, I investigated decellularization techniques for liver and cartilage tissues, aiming to minimize damage to the extracellular matrix. So far, this work suggests 3D-printed artificial cartilage with type I collagen, potentially aided by natural anti-inflammatory agents like Boswellic acid, could be a future alternative for cartilage implants.

  • Cell Therapy (2021-present)

Utilized mesenchymal stem cells and stem cell-derived exosomes to develop therapeutic approaches for liver and cartilage regeneration, with a focus on optimizing cell-based repair and regeneration techniques.

  • Systematic Reviews and Meta-analysis (2019-present)

Designed and managed research investigation of pathogens associated with human morbidity and mortality and potential therapeutic approaches in mitigating diverse stressors, including metabolic disorders and physical and chemical stress factors.

  • Conducting Surveys (2021-2023)

In two international projects, I developed an online survey that asked the participants whether they contracted COVID-19, their symptoms, time to recover, vaccination stage, and their frequency of eating various types of foods/beverages. The survey was developed in 10 different languages.
